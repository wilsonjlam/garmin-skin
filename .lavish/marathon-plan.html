The approach

Four levers, in priority order

We are not chasing speed — you have it. Every lever below builds the thing you were missing: the capacity to run all 26.2 strong.

01

Consistency over heroics

A protected weekly floor matters more than any peak. The goal: never have a "crash week" again. Build to 5 run days so volume comes from frequency, not punishing single runs.[A3]

02

Long-run volume + fueling

Multiple runs of 16–20 mi, each a rehearsal: carbs every 30–40 min, race shoes, race breakfast. The wall at mile 17 is usually an empty fuel tank, not dead legs.

03

Strength for durability

1–2×/week, running-specific, with eccentric/quad work for the downhills and late hills. This is the single biggest change from your 2024 build.[A1]

04

Pacing discipline for NYC's hills

NYC is not flat Philly — five bridges and a Fifth Ave climb at mile 23. We rehearse running hills on tired legs and going out controlled.

You asked me to challenge this

"Should I aim for 40 mpw?"[A1]

Short answer: yes — ~40 is a good peak, but it's the wrong thing to fixate on. Here's the honest read of your data:

2024: ~30 mpw peak, but ragged

You hit 30+ only a handful of times, with crash weeks in between. The number wasn't the problem — the holes between the good weeks were.

2026: ~38–42 mpw, but solid

Most build weeks 30–38, peaking ~41 for one week. The win isn't "+10 miles" — it's that every week lands and the long runs stack.

If you ran a flawless 30 mpw block — consistent, with three 18–20 milers and strength — you'd beat your 2024 self comfortably. 40 mpw done well is even better, and it's within reach. So we'll target it, but if a week needs to give, we cut the peak, never the long run or the consistency.

The missing piece from 2024

Strength program

Running-specific, not bodybuilding. The priority is single-leg stability and eccentric/quad strength — the exact quality that fails when you're descending the Verrazzano or climbing Fifth Ave on dead legs. Keep heavy lifting in the base/build phases; shift to maintenance as mileage peaks.[A1]

Equipment default: dumbbells & kettlebells. Every main lift is written for DB/KB — no barbell needed. Each session has a "Level up later (barbell)" note for when you add the bar with a hand from your friend. Sessions are ~45 min: main move + 3–4 accessories.

How to use this

Warm-up (5–8 min, every session)

  1. 3–4 min easy bike or jog
  2. Leg swings — 10 front/back + 10 side, each leg
  3. 10 bodyweight squats + 10 glute bridges
  4. Main lift only: 2 build-up sets (light, then ~70% of working DBs/KB)

Progression rule

When you hit all sets & all reps with clean form at the target tempo/RPE, add load next time — grab the next DB/KB up, or add a rep per set until you can jump weight. If form or RPE breaks down, hold the load and earn it again.

The 48h rule

Never lift heavy in the 48 hours before the long run. Put hard lifting early in the week, away from quality run days. Lower legs should feel fresh, not trashed, going into long-run or workout days.

Cadence: Base & Build run A on Tue, B on Thu. At Peak drop to 1×/wk — do Strength A. Taper is light only (Phase 4). Rest 2–3 min on heavy main lifts; 60–90 s on accessories.

Joint resilience & mobility knees · ankles · hips

A 5–8 min routine for the achy knees/ankles/hips. Do it most days — as part of your lifting warm-up or on easy/rest days. Mix of strength-prehab (load builds resilient joints) and mobility (restore range). Achy-but-not-injured joints usually respond to consistent loading + mobility within a couple of weeks. Back off anything that turns sharp — achy/working is fine, sharp/pinchy is not.

Know the beast

NYC course strategy

Five bridges and a sting in the tail. Unlike Philadelphia, NYC punishes anyone who banks time early. The whole race is about arriving at mile 16 with your legs intact.

Start · SIBrooklynQueensManhattanBronx5th AveFinish

Approximate elevation profile (relative). Mile markers below.

Mile 1 — Verrazzano climb

A ~1-mile bridge climb right off the gun, then a fast descent. Don't power up it, don't bomb down it. Easy effort — the adrenaline trap is real.

Miles 2–13 — Brooklyn

Mostly flat, huge crowds, feels easy. This is where the race is lost. Hold goal pace or 5–10 s slower. No banking time.

Miles 15–16 — Queensboro Bridge

Long, quiet, no spectators — a mental low point and a real climb. Settle in, shorten your stride, grind it patiently.

Miles 16–18 — First Avenue

The crowd wall of noise hits as you come off the bridge. It will make you surge. Don't. This is exactly where you over-spent in Philly.

Miles 20–21 — Bronx / Willis Ave

Bridges and the classic "wall" zone. If you fueled all race, you arrive here with something left. Stay present, keep eating.

Miles 23–24 — Fifth Ave climb 🔑

A long gradual uphill into Central Park when your legs are dead. The crux of the race. This is what the strength work and tired-legs hill reps are for.

Race-day logistics (don't get caught out)

  • • Wave start, late morning. You'll wait hours at the Staten Island start village — bring throwaway warm layers + extra fuel.
  • • Nov 1 is cold. Dress for the wait, not just the run. Practice in cool-weather gear during October long runs.
  • • Long time on feet pre-gun. Have a fueling plan for the wait, not just the race.

Pacing plan: even-to-negative split

  • • First 10 mi: 5–10 s/mi slower than goal pace. Bank patience, not time.
  • • Miles 10–20: settle onto goal pace.
  • • Miles 20–26.2: whatever you have left. If you did this right, you're passing people on Fifth Ave instead of walking it.

What "success" looks like

Tiered goals

A — stretch
Sub-4:30

≈10:18/mi. Realistic if the block goes clean. Your engine already supports faster — this respects NYC's hills + your durability still being new.

B — primary

A1 Peak at 40 mi/wk & keep 2 strength sessions (periodized)

Decision: cap running at ~40 mi/wk peak; 2× strength in base/build, auto-drop to 1× at peak; 1× is the non-negotiable floor.

Why strength beats marginal miles for you
  • • Your limiter is durability, not the engine (see A2) — extra easy miles pad a strength you already have.
  • • Strength delivers stimulus miles can't: running economy ~2–4%, late-race economy/form retention (your mile-17 failure), and eccentric quad + hill force.
  • • NYC-specific: eccentric load on the Verrazzano descent; force for the Queensboro (mi 15) & Fifth Ave (mi 23) climbs.
The honest nuance
  • • 1×/week captures most of the benefit — the 2nd session is "better & faster," not "with-vs-without" (diminishing returns).
  • • Where volume legitimately wins: you're at 12 mi/wk — early miles are the highest-leverage input, and the long run is sacred.
  • • So the plan periodizes: build = miles + 2× strength while mileage is moderate; peak = 1× biased to eccentric work.

Verdict: 40 done consistently > 40+ done raggedly. The marginal 10–15 miles are "more of what you already have"; the strength is a stimulus you get nowhere else in the plan.
