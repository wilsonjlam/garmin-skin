# NYC Marathon 2026 Training Plan — Wilson

## Context

You're running the **2026 TCS NYC Marathon on Sunday, November 1, 2026** (confirmed — first Sunday of November, 50th anniversary of the five-borough course). Today is June 28, 2026, giving an **18-week build**.

Last marathon (Philadelphia, Nov 2024) you ran **4:55**, walking from mile 17, and it hurt. The goal this time: **finish strong, no walking**, time secondary (a realistic byproduct is ~4:35–4:45).

### What your Garmin data actually shows (this drives the plan)

**2024 Philly block (Apr–Nov 2024):**
- You peaked at **~37 mi/week**, not 30 — with several 30–34 mi weeks. The number wasn't your problem.
- The block was **very spiky**: missed full weeks (W18) and near-zero weeks dropped in mid-build (W41: 5 mi, W43: 8 mi), then big jumps. **Inconsistency was the #1 issue.**
- Long runs were run at **~11:00/mi — essentially your race pace**. No easy/hard separation, so easy days were too hard and long runs didn't build aerobic depth efficiently.
- Race: 11:05/mi avg, **HR avg 156 / max 177** — your heart was *not* maxed. Combined with you reporting **legs/cramping** as the first failure at mile 17, this was a **muscular-endurance + pacing + fueling fade, not a cardio blowup.**

**Last 60 days (current fitness):**
- ~10–16 mi/week, longest run **7.25 mi**, easy pace 10:00–10:45/mi at **HR 150–160** (too high for "easy"). A modest but usable base.

### On your 40 mpw target (you asked me to challenge it)
40 is a fine **peak** — only ~+10% over your true 2024 peak of 37. But fixating on it misreads why Philly hurt. You already trained at 37 and still cramped/walked. The levers that actually fix the mile-17 wall, in order: **(1) consistency — never miss weeks; (2) run easy runs genuinely easy so long runs build durability; (3) practice race fueling/electrolytes on long runs; (4) strength + pacing discipline for NYC's late hills.** This plan builds to 40 as the *output* of consistency, not the target itself.

### Why NYC specifically shapes this plan
The course is built to punish a mile-17 fader: Verrazzano Bridge climb in miles 1–2 (don't bank time here), the long **Queensboro Bridge climb at mile 15–16**, then **rolling uphill through miles 23–26** (5th Ave into Central Park) — exactly where you died last time. So: **hill work, downhill practice, and strong-finish long runs are non-negotiable.**

- **Long runs:** start easy (11:15–11:45), with later long runs finishing the last 3–6 mi at MP.
- **Hill/tempo:** by effort (comfortably hard), not a fixed pace.

## Strength Program (2×/week, full gym)

The direct antidote to muscular cramping/fade. Lift after runs or on the prescribed days; **back off loads in W16–18 taper** (keep movement, cut volume ~50%, stop heavy in race week).

**Strength A — Wed (heavy lower body, 45–55 min)**
- Back squat — 3×5–8
- Romanian deadlift — 3×8
- Walking lunges (loaded) — 3×10/leg ← marathon-leg durability
- **Eccentric calf raises — 3×12–15** ← directly targets late-race calf cramps + bridge/downhill load
- Plank + side plank — 2×30–45s each

**Strength B — Fri (single-leg / plyo / core, lighter, 35–45 min)**
- Bulgarian split squat — 3×8/leg
- Single-leg RDL — 3×8/leg
- Hip thrust — 3×10
- Step-ups — 3×10/leg
- Low-volume plyo (box jumps or pogo hops) — 3×6 (skip in taper) ← running economy/stiffness
- Core: dead bugs, bird dogs, Pallof press — 2 rounds

Progress A by adding small load every 1–2 weeks through W12, then maintain.

## Fueling, Electrolytes & Pacing (where Philly was lost)

**Fueling (practice on every long run ≥10 mi):**
- **30–60g carbs/hour** from gels/chews, starting by mile 4–5, not when you feel empty.
- Race day target: a gel every ~30–40 min. Your W13/W15 long runs are full dress rehearsals.

**Electrolytes (your cramping lever):**
- Summer training = high sweat/sodium loss. Use an electrolyte mix on long runs and dial in **sodium intake** (aim ~500–800 mg/hr in heat; find your tolerance in training).
- This plus strength is your two-front attack on cramping.

**Pacing for NYC (no-walk insurance):**
- **Negative split.** Run miles 1–13 at MP or 5–10 sec slower. The Verrazzano start is downhill-then-up — **do not bank time**; cramps come from early overpace.
- Respect the **Queensboro climb (mile 15–16)** — ease effort, hold pace on the descent into 1st Ave.
- Save something for the **miles 23–26 uphill grind**. If you've got legs left there, you've already beaten 2024.
- Practice **downhill running** in long runs (controlled, quick cadence) so the bridge descents don't trash your quads.

## Verification / how to know it's working

Check these in Garmin every ~3 weeks — they confirm the plan is landing before race day proves it:
1. **Easy-run HR dropping at the same pace** — e.g., 10:30/mi that cost HR 158 in June should cost ~148 by September. This is aerobic base improving (your missing 2024 ingredient).
2. **Consistency**: zero missed weeks; weekly mileage tracks the table within ~10%. Compare against the spiky 2024 chart — smooth is the win.
3. **Long-run "MP finish" feels controlled**, not desperate — especially W11/W15 when you close at 10:30–10:45/mi.
4. **No cramping in the W13/W15 long runs** when fueled + electrolyted as planned. If you cramp in training, we adjust sodium/strength before race day.
5. Garmin **VO2max / race predictor** trending toward sub-4:45 by October is a good corroborating signal (secondary to how the runs feel).

If any check is off (HR not dropping, cramps persist, niggles appear), we hold or repeat a week rather than push — **consistency beats heroics** is the whole thesis of this plan.
